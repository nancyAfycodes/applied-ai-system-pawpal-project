# Bichon Frise — Breed-Specific Care Guidelines

## Exercise
- Bichon Frises are moderately active and require 30–45 minutes of exercise daily.
- Short walks and indoor play sessions are ideal — they adapt well to apartment living.
- Despite their small size, they enjoy agility games and interactive play.
- Avoid over-exercising in extreme heat — their small size makes them more susceptible to overheating.

## Feeding
- Adult Bichon Frises typically require ¼ to ¾ cup of high-quality dry food daily, split into two meals.
- They are prone to food allergies and sensitive stomachs — grain-free or limited-ingredient diets are often recommended.
- Avoid table scraps as Bichons are prone to obesity despite their small size.
- Fresh water should always be available.

## Health Considerations
- Bichon Frises are prone to bladder stones — ensure adequate hydration and a vet-approved diet.
- They are known to suffer from allergies (environmental and food) — monitor for itching, ear infections, and tear staining.
- Dental disease is common in small breeds — daily tooth brushing or dental treats are essential.
- Their hypoallergenic coat does not shed much but requires regular professional grooming every 4–6 weeks.

## Temperament and Enrichment
- Bichon Frises are highly social and do not tolerate being left alone for long periods — separation anxiety is common.
- They thrive on human interaction and are excellent companion dogs.
- Mental stimulation through training, puzzle toys, and short trick sessions keeps them happy.
- They respond exceptionally well to positive reinforcement — harsh correction causes stress.

## Scheduling Notes
- Schedule two short walks daily — morning and afternoon are ideal.
- Include a daily dental care task.
- Mental enrichment should be scheduled at least once daily to prevent boredom.
- Professional grooming should be noted as a monthly recurring task.
