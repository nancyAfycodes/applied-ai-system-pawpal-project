# Golden Retriever — Breed-Specific Care Guidelines

## Exercise
- Golden Retrievers are high-energy dogs requiring 60–90 minutes of exercise daily.
- They excel at fetch, swimming, and trail walking — activities that engage both body and mind.
- Without adequate exercise, Golden Retrievers are prone to destructive behavior and anxiety.
- Puppies should avoid high-impact exercise (jumping, long runs) until 18 months to protect developing joints.

## Feeding
- Adult Golden Retrievers typically require 3–5 cups of high-quality dry food daily, split into two meals.
- They are prone to obesity — monitor portion sizes carefully and avoid free feeding.
- Avoid feeding immediately before or after exercise to reduce risk of bloat (GDV).
- Fish oil supplements are particularly beneficial for Golden Retrievers to support coat and joint health.

## Health Considerations
- Golden Retrievers are predisposed to hip and elbow dysplasia — regular low-impact exercise and joint supplements are recommended.
- They have a higher-than-average risk of certain cancers — annual vet checkups are essential.
- Their dense double coat requires regular brushing (3–4 times per week) to prevent matting and reduce shedding.
- Ear infections are common due to floppy ears — weekly ear checks and cleaning are recommended.

## Temperament and Enrichment
- Golden Retrievers are highly social and do not do well with prolonged isolation.
- They respond exceptionally well to positive reinforcement training.
- Mental enrichment (puzzle feeders, training sessions, scent games) is as important as physical exercise.
- They tend to be mouthy as puppies — chew toys and training should be prioritized early.

## Scheduling Notes
- Prioritize two exercise sessions daily — morning and evening are ideal.
- Schedule grooming sessions at least twice per week.
- Mental enrichment tasks should be included at least once daily.
