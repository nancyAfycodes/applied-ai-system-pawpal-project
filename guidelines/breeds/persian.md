# Persian — Breed-Specific Care Guidelines

## Feeding
- Adult Persians should be fed twice daily — morning and evening.
- They do well on wet food diets which support urinary tract health and hydration.
- Persian cats are prone to kidney disease — a low-phosphorus diet may be recommended by a vet.
- Their flat face (brachycephalic) can make eating from deep bowls difficult — use flat or shallow dishes.
- Fresh water should always be available — a pet fountain is recommended as Persians can be reluctant drinkers.

## Grooming
- Persians have a long, thick coat that requires daily brushing to prevent matting and tangles.
- Professional grooming every 4–6 weeks is strongly recommended.
- Their flat faces cause tear overflow — wipe around the eyes daily to prevent staining and infection.
- Ear cleaning should be done weekly as Persians are prone to ear wax buildup.
- Dental care is important — brush teeth several times per week or provide dental treats.

## Health Considerations
- Persians are brachycephalic (flat-faced) — they can have breathing difficulties in heat or stress.
- They are prone to Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD) — regular vet checkups and bloodwork are essential.
- Eye conditions are common due to their facial structure — daily eye cleaning is non-negotiable.
- Keep them indoors — their long coat and flat face make them poorly suited for outdoor life.
- They are prone to hairballs — hairball remedy treats or gel should be given 2–3 times per week.

## Temperament and Enrichment
- Persians are calm, gentle, and affectionate — they prefer quiet environments.
- They are not highly active but enjoy gentle play sessions of 10–15 minutes twice daily.
- Avoid loud or chaotic environments — stress can trigger health issues in Persians.
- They enjoy soft toys, feather wands, and gentle interactive play.
- Persians are creatures of habit — sudden routine changes cause significant stress.

## Scheduling Notes
- Daily grooming is non-negotiable — schedule a brushing task every morning.
- Daily eye cleaning should be a high priority task.
- Feeding times must be consistent — use shallow dishes.
- Schedule hairball remedy 2–3 times per week as a routine medication task.
- Keep all activity tasks gentle and short — 10–15 minutes maximum.
