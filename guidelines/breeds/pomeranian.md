# Pomeranian — Breed-Specific Care Guidelines

## Exercise
- Pomeranians are energetic for their size and require 20–30 minutes of exercise daily.
- Short walks twice daily combined with indoor play sessions are ideal.
- Despite their energy, they tire quickly — avoid overly long or strenuous sessions.
- They are sensitive to heat — walk during cooler morning or evening hours in summer.

## Feeding
- Adult Pomeranians typically require ¼ to ½ cup of high-quality dry food daily, split into two meals.
- Small breed formulas are recommended to support their fast metabolism.
- They are prone to hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) — consistent meal timing is critical.
- Avoid skipping meals, especially in puppies — hypoglycemia can be dangerous.
- Fresh water should always be available.

## Health Considerations
- Pomeranians are prone to luxating patella (knee dislocation) — avoid jumping from high surfaces.
- Dental disease is very common — daily brushing or dental chews are essential.
- They are prone to tracheal collapse — use a harness instead of a collar for walks.
- Their thick double coat requires brushing 2–3 times per week to prevent matting.
- Alopecia X (coat loss) is a known breed condition — monitor coat health closely.

## Temperament and Enrichment
- Pomeranians are alert, curious, and intelligent — they love to learn new tricks.
- They can develop small dog syndrome if not given consistent boundaries.
- Mental enrichment through training sessions and puzzle feeders is important daily.
- They can be vocal — training to manage barking should be part of their routine.
- Despite their small size, they have bold personalities and do not always recognize their size limitations around larger dogs.

## Scheduling Notes
- Schedule two short walks daily — use a harness, never a collar.
- Include a daily dental care task.
- Meal times must be consistent — never skip to avoid hypoglycemia risk.
- Brushing should be scheduled 2–3 times per week.
