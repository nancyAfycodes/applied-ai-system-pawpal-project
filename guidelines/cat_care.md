# Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding
- Adult cats should be fed twice daily — once in the morning and once in the evening.
- Kittens (under 6 months) require three to four small meals per day.
- Fresh water should always be available — cats are prone to dehydration.
- Wet food is recommended at least once daily to support urinary tract health.
- Avoid leaving dry food out all day as it can lead to overeating.
- Cats with sensitive stomachs should be on grain-free or limited-ingredient diets.

## Litter Box Care
- Litter boxes should be scooped at least once daily, ideally twice.
- A full litter change should occur every one to two weeks.
- Provide one litter box per cat plus one extra (e.g., two cats = three boxes).
- Place litter boxes in quiet, low-traffic areas away from food and water.
- Changes in litter box habits may indicate health issues — monitor closely.

## Medications and Supplements
- Daily vitamins and supplements should be given at the same time each day.
- Medications prescribed by a vet should never be skipped or doubled up.
- Hairball remedies should be given two to three times per week for long-haired cats.
- Flea and tick prevention should be administered monthly.
- Always log medication times to track compliance.

## Grooming
- Short-haired cats require brushing once a week.
- Long-haired cats require brushing daily to prevent matting.
- Nail trimming should occur every two to three weeks.
- Dental care (brushing or dental treats) should occur several times per week.
- Bathing is rarely necessary unless directed by a vet.

## Enrichment and Mental Health
- Cats are crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk.
- Interactive play sessions of 10–15 minutes twice daily support mental health.
- Provide vertical spaces (cat trees, shelves) for climbing and observation.
- Window perches are excellent low-effort enrichment tools.
- Cats benefit from routine — sudden schedule changes can cause stress.

## Scheduling Priorities
- High priority: feeding, litter box cleaning, medications
- Medium priority: playtime, grooming, enrichment
- Low priority: optional treats, window time
- Never skip high priority tasks — flag them immediately if they cannot be scheduled.
