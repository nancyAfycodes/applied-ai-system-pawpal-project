# Dog Care Guidelines

## Feeding
- Adult dogs should be fed twice daily — once in the morning and once in the evening.
- Puppies (under 6 months) require three to four small meals per day.
- Fresh water should always be available.
- Avoid feeding dogs within 1 hour before or after vigorous exercise to prevent bloat.
- Grain-free diets should only be used under veterinary guidance.

## Exercise
- Adult dogs require a minimum of 30 minutes of exercise twice daily.
- High-energy breeds (e.g., Golden Retrievers, Border Collies) may need 60–90 minutes per day.
- Morning walks are recommended before feeding to establish routine.
- Avoid strenuous exercise in extreme heat (above 85°F / 29°C).
- Puppies should follow the "5-minute rule" — 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, twice daily.

## Medications and Supplements
- Daily vitamins and supplements should be given at the same time each day for consistency.
- Medications prescribed by a vet should never be skipped or doubled up.
- Fish oil supplements are best given with meals to aid absorption.
- Flea and tick prevention should be administered monthly.
- Always log medication times to track compliance.

## Grooming
- Short-haired breeds require brushing once a week.
- Long-haired breeds require brushing three to four times per week.
- Bathing should occur every four to six weeks unless directed otherwise by a vet.
- Nail trimming should occur every three to four weeks.
- Ear cleaning should be done weekly for floppy-eared breeds.

## Enrichment and Mental Health
- Dogs thrive on routine — keeping tasks at consistent times reduces anxiety.
- Mental enrichment (puzzle feeders, training sessions) should complement physical exercise.
- Social interaction with humans or other dogs is important for emotional wellbeing.
- Puppies and senior dogs may need more rest periods between activities.

## Scheduling Priorities
- High priority: feeding, morning walk, medications
- Medium priority: afternoon walk, grooming, enrichment
- Low priority: treats, optional playtime
- Never skip high priority tasks — flag them immediately if they cannot be scheduled.
