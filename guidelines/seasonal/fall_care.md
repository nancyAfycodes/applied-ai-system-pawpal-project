# Fall Pet Care Guidelines (September – November)

## General
- Fall brings cooler temperatures — a great time to increase outdoor activity duration.
- Shorter daylight hours mean evening walks may occur in darkness — use reflective gear.

## Health Alerts
- Mushrooms and fallen fruits in yards can be toxic — supervise outdoor time carefully.
- Rodenticide use increases in fall — keep pets away from areas where bait may be placed.
- Schedule annual vet checkup and vaccine updates before winter.
- Flea and tick prevention should continue through fall until first hard frost.

## Exercise
- Cooler temperatures make longer walks and outdoor play more comfortable.
- Great season for hiking and trail activities with dogs.
- Leaves can hide hazards on trails — monitor footing carefully.

## Grooming
- Fall coat growth begins — increase brushing to manage transition from summer to winter coat.
- Check for ticks after every outdoor excursion.

## Scheduling Notes
- Extend morning and evening walk durations as temperatures cool.
- Add a tick check task after each outdoor walk.
- Schedule annual vet appointment if not already done.
