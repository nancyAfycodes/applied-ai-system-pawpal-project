# Spring Pet Care Guidelines (March – May)

## General
- Spring is an excellent time to re-establish consistent outdoor routines after winter.
- Longer daylight hours allow for earlier morning walks and later evening activities.

## Health Alerts
- Flea and tick season begins in spring — ensure monthly prevention is up to date.
- Allergy season peaks in spring — monitor pets for itching, sneezing, or watery eyes.
- Spring plants (tulips, daffodils, azaleas) can be toxic to dogs and cats — supervise outdoor time.

## Exercise
- Gradually increase outdoor exercise duration after winter inactivity.
- Muddy conditions after rain — check and clean paws after every outdoor walk.

## Grooming
- Spring shedding season — increase brushing frequency to manage coat blowout.
- Schedule a professional grooming appointment if coat is heavily matted from winter.

## Scheduling Notes
- Add a paw cleaning task after each outdoor walk.
- Increase grooming frequency from weekly to 3–4 times per week during peak shedding.
