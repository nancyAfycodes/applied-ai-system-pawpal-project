# Summer Pet Care Guidelines (June – August)

## General
- Heat is the primary concern in summer — adjust schedules to avoid peak temperatures.
- Ensure fresh water is always available and replenished frequently in hot weather.

## Health Alerts
- Never leave pets in a parked car — temperatures can reach fatal levels within minutes.
- Watch for signs of heatstroke: excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, vomiting.
- Hot pavement can burn paw pads — walk during early morning or evening when ground is cooler.
- Flea, tick, and mosquito prevention is critical throughout summer.

## Exercise
- Shift walks to early morning (before 8am) or evening (after 6pm) to avoid heat.
- Reduce walk duration on days above 85°F (29°C).
- Swimming is an excellent low-impact summer exercise option for dogs.
- Cats should be kept indoors during peak heat hours.

## Hydration
- Add a hydration check task to the daily schedule.
- Consider a pet water fountain to encourage consistent drinking.

## Scheduling Notes
- Move morning walk earlier — before 8am is ideal.
- Add a midday hydration check task.
- Avoid scheduling outdoor exercise between 11am and 5pm on hot days.
