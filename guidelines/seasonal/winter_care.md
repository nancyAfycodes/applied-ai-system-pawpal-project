# Winter Pet Care Guidelines (December – February)

## General
- Cold weather requires adjusted schedules — shorter outdoor sessions and more indoor enrichment.
- Ensure pets have a warm, dry resting area away from drafts.

## Health Alerts
- Hypothermia and frostbite are real risks for pets in extreme cold.
- Rock salt and ice melt chemicals can irritate and burn paw pads — rinse paws after every walk.
- Antifreeze is extremely toxic to pets — clean up any spills immediately.
- Dry indoor air can cause skin and coat dryness — consider a humidifier.

## Exercise
- Shorten outdoor walk duration in freezing temperatures — especially for short-haired breeds.
- Replace some outdoor exercise with indoor play and enrichment activities.
- Dog coats or sweaters are recommended for short-haired breeds below 32°F (0°C).
- Cats should remain indoors in extreme cold.

## Grooming
- Dry indoor heating can cause static and coat dryness — increase moisturizing grooming products.
- Check paws after every outdoor walk for ice balls between toes or salt irritation.

## Scheduling Notes
- Shorten morning walk duration in freezing temperatures.
- Add a paw check and cleaning task after each outdoor walk.
- Replace one outdoor exercise session with an indoor enrichment task on extremely cold days.
